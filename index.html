Abstract

Self-driving cars use artificial intelligence, sensors, and advanced engineering to drive without human input. They rely on systems like LiDAR, radar, cameras, and machine learning algorithms to detect obstacles, recognize road markings, and make driving decisions. This article explains how autonomous vehicles understand their surroundings, process sensor data, and plan safe routes. It also covers engineering challenges, including combining data from multiple sensors, unpredictable human behaviour, bad weather, and ethical issues. The article explores current limitations and future developments, such as communication between vehicles and smarter AI models. This offers an accessible introduction to the science and engineering behind autonomous vehicles for Foundation Year students.
